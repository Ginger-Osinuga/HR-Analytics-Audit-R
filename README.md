# HR Analytics: Pay Equity & Performance Audit (R)
A statistical analysis of employee compensation and pay equity using R and the tidyverse.

### 📊 Project Overview
This project analyzes an HR dataset of 311 employees to identify trends in compensation, pay equity, and the relationship between performance and engagement.

### 🛠️ Tools Used
*   **Language:** R
*   **Environment:** RStudio (Posit Cloud)
*   **Libraries:** `tidyverse` (dplyr, ggplot2)

### 📈 Key Insights
*   **Pay Gap Identified:** Discovered a **$2,842** average salary difference between male ($70,629) and female ($67,787) employees.
*   **Compenation Red Flag:** Discovered that the **"Needs Improvement"** group has a slightly higher average salary ($68,408) than the **"Fully Meets"** group ($68,367), suggesting potential pay compression or misalignment in the merit increase process.
*   **Performance vs. Pay:** Employees rated as "Exceeds" earned the highest average salary ($77,145), while those on a "PIP" earned the lowest ($58,971).
*   **Engagement Paradox:** A scatter plot with a linear regression line showed a **flat trend**, suggesting that higher salaries do not strictly correlate with higher engagement scores in this organization.
